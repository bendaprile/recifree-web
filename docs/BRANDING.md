# Recifree Brand Identity Guidelines

## 1. Core Brand Persona
Recifree is the **"Culinary Ad-Blocker."** It is an open-source, populist rebellion against the bloated, ad-ridden recipe industry.
- **The Vibe:** It's an incredibly satisfying, frictionless utility.
- **The "Tried & True" Promise:** Recifree is a compilation of the *very tastiest* recipes, not a data-dump of untested meals. We champion the philosophy that every recipe on the site must be taste-tested and actively loved by the person adding it. 
- **Accounts for Utility:** We offer accounts solely to help you save and sync your kitchen collection. No newsletters, no cross-site tracking, no selling your data. We maintain a privacy-first ethos where an account is a tool for you, not a product for us.
- **Voice & Tone:** Witty, highly practical, and deeply respectful of the user's time. The tone should be slightly irreverent toward the "fluff" of traditional food blogs, but always stay appetizing, warm, and helpful when focusing on the actual food.

## 2. Copywriting & Slogans
The copy should immediately validate the user's frustration with modern recipe sites, delivering the punchline of our value proposition without sounding overly bitter or antagonistic.

**Core Slogan Concepts:**
*   "All flavor. No fluff."
*   "Recipes, refined."
*   "Your next meal, minus the noise."
*   "Just what you need to cook."

**Micro-copy (The Delighters):**
*   **Loading an extraction:** "Extracting the recipe, tossing the life story..." or "Bypassing the pop-ups..."
*   **Empty State (Saved Recipes):** "Your kitchen, zero pop-ups. Paste a link to start."
*   **Primary Call to Action:** Instead of standard "Submit", use "Extract Recipe" or "Strip the Fluff."

## 3. Visual Identity: Minimalist Editorial
The visual design must actively reject the cluttered, pastel, script-font aesthetic of standard food blogs, favoring extreme utility. 

*   **Color Palette:** Rather than diving fully into a "terminal/hacker" aesthetic (which can feel unappetizing), the palette should reflect "Minimalist Editorial Utility."
    *   **Backgrounds:** Crisp paper-whites or soft off-whites (like flour).
    *   **Text:** Deep slate or rich charcoal for maximum high-contrast readability.
    *   **Accents:** A vibrant, high-visibility "utility" color for buttons and interactive states—like a bright 'Safety Orange' (citrus) or an 'Apron Green' to bridge the gap between software tool and food.
*   **Typography:** The "Engineering UI" approach.
    *   **Data Structure:** Use a clean, monospaced font specifically for numbers, ingredient quantities, and timers (emulating a digital kitchen scale).
    *   **Reading Blocks:** Pair the monospace with a highly readable, modern sans-serif (e.g., Inter or Roboto) for instructions.
*   **Dark Mode:** A high-contrast dark mode is essential, appealing to the "extractor tool" vibe and preventing eye strain for late-night meal preppers.

## 4. Iconography and Mascot
A fun, memorable mascot bridges the gap between a sterile software tool and a beloved, shareable app.

*   **The Mascot:** A sleek, energetic "Extraction Helper." Rather than a trash-digging animal, the mascot should be an efficient little robot designed like a vintage kitchen appliance (e.g., a friendly smart-blender or a floating whisk).
*   **The Animation Hook:** The mascot is utilized during the loading phase. When a user pastes a URL, the mascot animates—visually "scanning" the URL, slicing away blocks of grey text with a laser or a chef's knife, and proudly presenting a glowing recipe card.
*   **The App Icon:** A minimalist line-drawing of a chef's knife cleanly slicing through a dense block of text, or a neat funnel distilling data into a single drop.
